## Grouped by failure category (per QA_RUNBOOK)

**Accessibility (1 systemic class, 2+ instances):** the readiness ("I'm not
100%") entry point and its entire follow-up sheet, plus "Missing equipment?",
all expose internal component-id strings as their accessible label instead
of the visible text. This is broader than the single-row gap found in the
Workout audit's Remove-exercise flow — here it's the row trigger *and* every
option in the sheet it opens. A screen-reader user would hear ids like
"readiness-option-sick-week" instead of "Sick / run down."

**Data persistence / visible-plan-mismatch (2):** (a) the "tired" readiness
signal submits with no confirmation and leaves `readiness-clear-state`
active afterward — same pattern as the Workout audit's session-feedback
finding, now seen on the Program/Home screen too, suggesting this may be a
class of bug rather than a one-off. (b) After a season-phase shift that
visibly rebuilt the whole week, the phase-status card itself did not update
— it still offers to do a shift that (per the rebuilt week) already happened.

**Rules-engine / scope boundary (1):** "Busy or away this week?" is the only
Group-5-adjacent control on this screen that isn't a native tap-sheet flow —
it routes into Coach chat. Left untested past that observation per the
project's explicit Coach Architecture Escalation Rule (CLAUDE.md) and Group
6's "mutations blocked pending reassessment."

**Known pre-existing gap, confirmed not re-reported (1):** equipment preset
selection ("Dumbbells only") did not change Monday's Back Squat prescription
— matches `FLOW_AUDIT_2026-07-07.md`'s already-logged "equipment not
consumed by generation" gap.

**Observation, not scored (1):** the off-season rebuild also removed the
"+ Team Training" tag from Tuesday and Thursday entirely (both days changed
type to "Recovery Conditioning"). This may be intentional off-season
behavior (no club team training in that phase) rather than a bug; flagging
for whoever owns the phase-shift rules rather than asserting a verdict,
since the Program audit treated "recurring team schedule untouched" as an
invariant in a different context (Bin action) and this may or may not be
the same invariant during a full-phase rebuild.

**Not tested:** "Repeat this week into next week" (adjacent convenience
action, not in the Group 1-6 contract) and pass criterion #5 (survives app
relaunch, skipped per the documented white-screen-on-relaunch environment
fact).
